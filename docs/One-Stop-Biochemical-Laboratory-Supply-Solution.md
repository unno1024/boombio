Common Pain Points of Global Laboratory & Industrial Procurement
Fragmented supply chains have always restricted the efficiency of scientific research and industrial manufacturing. Most laboratories and enterprises face the following core problems:
1. Multiple Suppliers & Complicated Procurement Processes
Traditional lab procurement requires cooperation with dozens of suppliers for reagents, standard products, culture media, and consumables. Multi-vendor management leads to repeated communication, scattered orders, inconsistent after-sales services, and extremely low overall operational efficiency.
2. Unstable Product Quality & Risk of Experimental Failure
Experimental data accuracy depends entirely on the purity, activity, and batch consistency of raw materials. Unqualified biochemical reagents, impure standard substances, and contaminated laboratory consumables will directly lead to invalid experimental data, repeated tests, delayed project progress, and increased R&D costs.
3. Cross-Border Logistics Delays & Stock Shortages
Special biochemical products, microbial culture media, and new energy battery materials have high transportation thresholds, requiring cold chain logistics, dangerous goods declaration, and commodity inspection procedures. Ordinary suppliers cannot support standardized cross-border delivery, resulting in frequent stockouts and project suspensions.
4. Lack of Professional Technical Support & Customized Solutions
Most raw material suppliers only provide sales services without professional scientific research guidance. For customized standard solutions, special culture media formulas, and battery material parameter adjustments, it is difficult to obtain targeted technical support, which cannot meet personalized R&D and testing needs.
5. Uncontrollable Costs & Resource Waste
Dispersed procurement cannot obtain bulk discount advantages, and irregular inventory management leads to reagent expiration, repeated purchases, and resource waste, greatly increasing the operating costs of laboratories and enterprises.
